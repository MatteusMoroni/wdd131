Espírito Santo, Brazil

Espírito Santo is a small coastal state in southeastern Brazil, known for its beautiful beaches like Guarapari and Itaúnas, lush Atlantic Forest, and mountainous regions such as Pedra Azul. Its capital, Vitória, is an island city with ports vital to Brazil’s economy. The state is famous for its moqueca capixaba (fish stew without dendê oil), Italian-influenced inland towns, and rich coffee production. Espírito Santo blends nature, culture, and industry in a compact, diverse territory.
